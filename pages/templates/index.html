What is the Cloud? What is Cloud Computing?

Posted by Yemi at 9:30 AM in General

The late Steve Jobs once said, “I don’t need a hard disk in my computer if I can get to the server faster.” The year was 1997, the internet itself was still in its infancy, and there was the Apple Co-Founder and longtime visionary talking about our future in something called the cloud.
That term and this idea of storing and hosting data on a connected network of virtual machines rather than hard disks has grown in popularity since, just as he predicted: “Carrying around these non-connected computers is byzantine by comparison.” Here, we cover all the questions one could possibly have about the concept of cloud server hosting — from the cloud infrastructure to the cost and features to look for and beyond.

Cloud servers are just virtual machines that run on what’s called a cloud computing environment. Rather than having a lone physical server, which is susceptible to hardware failure, cloud-hosted servers communicate over a network of connected servers — they’re all online, but none of your data is stored on a single hard drive.

Best SSD Web Hosting.

Posted by Yemi at 11:50 PM in News

When I decided it was finally time (past time, actually) to switch my hosting plan to something better, there was one specific feature I wanted — SSDs. My sites were crawling along painfully slowly, and I’d had enough.
We write plenty about how speed sells and performance is pivotal, but I wasn’t practicing what I preached. I had prioritized price over performance when shopping for my last hosting plan and wanted more for my modest collection of websites.
SSDs, which stand for solid-state drives, are an improved type of storage device that copies, writes, and opens files many times faster than traditional hard-disk drives, or HDDs. As the name implies, SSDs contain no moving parts; data is stored in flash-memory microchips, meaning the drives draw less power, create less noise, generate less heat, and last much longer than HDDs.
Because SSDs are a newer technology (gaining traction and popularity in the early 1990s, as opposed to when HDDs were introduced in the mid-1950s), they are more expensive and typically have less capacity than HDDs. While that doesn’t directly affect your hosting experience, you’ll understand why plans with SSDs tend to be pricier, come with lower storage limits — and still appeal to more seasoned web hosting customers.
